Good morning.
An AI agent at Meta held valid credentials, operated inside authorized boundaries, passed every identity check -- and still exposed sensitive data to employees who shouldn't have seen it. Meta confirmed the incident to The Information on March 18. Louis Columbus has the full analysis: four gaps in enterprise IAM that made it possible, four vendors now shipping controls against it, and the Saviynt survey showing 47% of CISOs have already watched an agent go rogue. Only 5% think they can stop one.
Meanwhile, Taryn Plumb's writeup of the latest Beyond the Pilot podcast covers the opposite problem -- enterprises racing to give AI deeper access to their users. Zoom AI Companion now tracks opinion divergence in meetings, not just action items. Read those two stories back to back.
Plus: Cursor launched Composer 2 -- 17-point jump on CursorBench, $0.50/M input tokens. OpenAI is acquiring the team behind Python's uv and ruff. And Google started beta-testing a native Gemini Mac app that can read your screen.
